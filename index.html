Earth Science with Mr. Porter

This school year we will be working together to advance your understanding of laboratory safety, scientific thinking and practice, the structure of the earth and its history, weather and climate, the causes of our seasons, and the structure of the universe and its contents.

Expectations

For each activity, I will provide safety equipment and let you know what you need to do to stay safe. Beyond safety, I have two important expectations for our daily activities:

Work Hard &
Be Nice to People

These expectations are simple and I know that every student is capable of meeting them.

Classwork & Grades

As long as the above expectations are met, the vast majority of work will be done in class. We will conduct laboratory activities, develop new vocabulary, and read interesting articles and books about the topics we are studying.

Your grade is based on your demonstrated understanding of the topics we are studying and the skills you are developing. Generally, the longer an activity takes to complete, the greater the number of points it is worth toward your final grade. Grades are a percentage of the total number of possible points. Grades are posted weekly in the classroom and recorded on a progress graph in the science section of your binder.

Late work can be turned in for up to 80% credit until the last two weeks of each quarter.

Class Content

Laboratory Practices

For the first few weeks of school we will focus on scientific processes, systems of measurement and laboratory safety. Please read with your parents and sign the laboratory safety contract; you cannot participate in laboratory activities if the form is not turned in.

Geology

The remainder of the fall semester we will study geology. This includes minerals, rocks, earthquakes and volcanoes.

Meteorology

Spring semester we will study weather and climate. A lot of time will be used creating and using tools that can be used to measure atmospheric conditions.

Astronomy

Toward the end of the school year we will start studying astronomy. We will model the solar system to scale, learn how stars form and how exoplanets are being discovered.
